ABOUT ME

Engineer | Curious Mind | Student

When someone tells you that they hate to be bad at things, what do you imagine?

I imagine hundreds of hours of hard work. Pushing through book after book to learn to code. Running thousands of miles in training. Patiently rehearsing strokes of a pen, a pencil, an airbrush. Late nights and early mornings. Time sacrificed in the persuit of perfection that may never come.

I imagine a person stuck without a solution, simmering for a little idea to march towards a breakthrough.

I've never been the type of person who thought it acceptable to stop progressing at anything worth doing. Because of my drive to be continously learning, I've managed to teach myself to excel at many things. This website is a perfect example; composed in Django, it uses a mixture of class-based generic views, the admin interface, 3rd party packages, custom code, the REST api framework, and more to demonstrate that I can learn complex systems and put them together into a finished project. The articles below are editable by a user with appropriate permissions, the admin is accessable to a superuser. The front page itself is a single page app which interfaces with a REST api through javascript.

Please explore some of the things I've created, and keep in mind that the type of person who trains 400 hours a year to run 100 mile races, or learns to draw just to spend 300 hours meticulously hand painting a motorcycle; that person takes the soft skills which enables them to succeed to work with them every day. They're not just qualified to do the job you're hiring for, they're qualified for the job that you didn't know you were going to need.
